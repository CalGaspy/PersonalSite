Jan 2025 - Present

Undergraduate Research Assistant | Drone Systems & Robotics – Dr. Venki's Lab

As an undergraduate researcher in Dr. Venki’s robotics lab, I’ve had the chance to design, build, and test complete robotic systems from the ground up. I’ve worked on motion platforms using stepper motors, linear rails, and sensors, wiring everything so the system could move safely and accurately. I also built aerial systems by soldering power boards, integrating GPS units, cameras, and flight controllers, and organizing all of the signal and power wiring. To troubleshoot issues, I relied on tools like multimeters and oscilloscopes to track down bad connections, debug signals, and make sure everything was working the way it should. On the software side, I configured flight-control settings, calibrated sensors, tuned movement parameters, and ran tests until the systems were stable and reliable. Overall, I’ve gotten hands-on experience with the full workflow CAD design, soldering, wiring, programming, and testing which has taught me how to bring complex robotic platforms together in a practical, real-world lab environment.

Summer 2024

Computer Engineering Intern – NV Energy (Nevada's Power Company)

As a Computer Engineering Intern at NV Energy, I took the initiative to develop a custom Management of Change (MOC) application used across 18 power plants, including both solar and natural gas facilities. I built the app using Microsoft Power Apps, Power FX, SharePoint, and Outlook, creating a system that made it easier for engineers and plant staff to track changes, manage approvals, and stay aligned with safety and maintenance procedures. To make sure the tool met real operational needs, I regularly visited power plants, spoke directly with engineers and technicians, and applied what I learned about day-to-day workflows into the app’s design. I also took the initiative to step outside my core role and was mentored in welding inspection and power plant maintenance inspections, which gave me a deeper understanding of mechanical systems and field safety standards. This role gave me the chance to take ownership of a project with real impact, while also gaining hands-on experience in how power is generated and maintained at scale.

August 2023 – June 2024

Event Coordinator - UNLV Satelite Club

As the Event Coordinator for the Rebel Satellite Club, I took the initiative to organize and lead weekly visits to elementary schools in our community. I worked with a team of three engineering students, and together we presented to classrooms of 30 to 40 kids at each school. Our goal was to teach students about space exploration, satellites, and basic physics in a way that was fun and easy to understand. I created PowerPoint presentations with colorful visuals to help explain the topics, and I also took the lead in designing and 3D-printing cube satellite models so the students could see and hold a real example of what we were talking about. To make our lessons even more engaging, we used balloons and string to perform hands-on rocket demos, helping the kids visualize how rockets launch and move. This role helped spark curiosity and excitement about science and engineering in young students.
